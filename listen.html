Music was consumed by the general public Purely through live performances in taverns or theatres
A Notable performance in 1600 jacopo peris euridice was said to be the birth of opera, premiered in Florence italy
Tells the myth of orpheus and euridice from the books of ovids metamorphes
Street music was also very prominent at this time. Performers would travel from city to city sharing their music and talent
Cities in italy including Venice, Rome and naples are known for their abundance of musical performances around this time.
Venice was home to a variety of church music, as were most cities at this time.
Notable composers inside this subgenre include st marks basilica which was known for its polychoral style of music.
Polychoral music meant that it had multiple choirs of singers and instruments performing at the same time.
This city was also home to the gabrieli family which also used this form of performance. Using double and even triple choirs in respective playings.
Rome also housed many talented musicians in the roman school, a group of composers with connections to the vatican, spread their church music to the general public in the renisance up to the early baroque period
