There were many big composers in the baroque era, here are just a notable few.

Claudio monteverdi (1567-1643)

Taught by marc’antonio ingegneri
Cremona italy
1592 he was appointed as a violin player to duke vincenzo I of mantua
His multiple journeys with monteverdi made it seem like his importance was increasing, someone else was offered de werts post upon its vacancy (his death) in 1596
Dissatisfied with his situation, upon the dukes death he decided to leave the court accepting maestro di cappella of st. marks in venice in 1613
He went on and wrote some of the most influential compositions in early baroque including 1610s vespro della beata\e vergine (vespers of the blessed virgin)
He also published 9 books of madrigals between 1587 and 1651

Heinrich schutz (1585-1672)

germany
Studied music with his innkeeper father
Landgrave moritz von hessenkassel heard 13 year old heinrich singing and asked his father to be in charge of his music career
Heinrich graduated with honors in 1608
Langrave gave heinrich a grant to travel to venice where he studied composition until 1913
Heinrich became court composer for the elector of saxony in dresden in 1615.
Heinrich was in this position for the rest of his career
During the 30 years war he studied with claudio monteverdi in venice and served as kapellmeisterto king christian IV of denmark for several years
Heinrich is credited with bring the italianate style to germany due to his studies in italy

Jean-Baptiste Lully (1632-1687)

Lully was one of the most popular Italian Baroque composers.
He was born into nobility.
He arrived in Paris in 1646.
He was working in the Court of Louis XIV in 1653 as instrumental composer and dancer.
Once he secured his spot as superintendent of music in 1661, he began writing comédies-ballets with playwright Moliére, combining italian pastoral opera with french ballet du cour.
In 1672 he got a license for Académie de musique, and multiple patents allowing him to control who used music in stages in other theater troupes.
His style was French opera in five acts including ballet, chorus and over the top set.
The style of these sets showed the way of life in Louis XIVs court perfectly.
